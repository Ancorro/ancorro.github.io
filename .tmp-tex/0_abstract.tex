\begin{abstract}
We evaluate whether representations from the ESM3 protein foundation model
transfer to sequence-only modeling of HIV broadly neutralizing antibodies
(bnAbs), and if domain adaptation via parameter-efficient fine-tuning alters
this transfer. We partition heavy-chain sequences into lineage groups and
evaluate frozen ESM3 and domain-adapted representations on classification and
regression tasks. Our results demonstrate that frozen ESM3 representations
effectively predict bnAb neutralization breadth and classify broad versus narrow
neutralizers, outperforming handcrafted baseline features. However, unsupervised
domain adaptation of the foundation model on antibody repertoires does not yield
significant performance improvements. We also evaluate representation
interpolation along lineage maturation pathways, showing that raw
embedding-space interpolation is comparable to specialized variational
autoencoder paths. These findings support frozen ESM3 as a viable exploratory
representation for antibody modeling, while highlighting the importance of
robust validation controls to avoid overstating performance gains.
\end{abstract}
